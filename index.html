Smart City Initiatives

The Department of Economic Innovation and Development is a pioneer in promoting future-ready initiatives in Miami, actively driving smart city initiatives across various sectors.

Through events like the annual Future of Mobility Summit, the department brings together thought leaders and industry experts to explore cutting-edge solutions in transportation and urban mobility.

Additionally, the department supports smart city initiatives by global technology leaders such as Dell and NVIDIA, collaborating on projects like the Brickell Digital Twin Demonstration Project, which uses Dell’s Computer Vision AI Model and NVIDIA Omniverse to analyze the impact of bridge operations on traffic.

The department is also backing the development of a 5G Innovation District in Little River and Little Haiti, further enhancing the city's connectivity and digital infrastructure.

These efforts are crucial in positioning Miami as a hub for innovation, fostering a city that is not only ready for the future but actively shaping it.

Economic Growth

Attracting investments, fostering entrepreneurship, and strengthening the local economy through infrastructure and incentives are key priorities for the Department of Economic Innovation and Development.

The department drives economic growth by supporting small businesses and startups, partnering with industry leaders like Google, AWS, JPMorgan, and Justworks to host networking events and foster collaboration.

Through the Built in Miami Program, a pre-accelerator initiative, and monthly tech mixers at Citizen M, the department provides resources and opportunities for entrepreneurs

Additionally, the department empowers women in business through the YouBelong meetups and supports Latin founders in collaboration with Endeavor Miami.

Workforce Development

Workforce development plays a vital role in Miami’s future.

The City of Miami invests heavily in supporting residents through scholarships and workforce development programs.

Each year, the city hosts the Venture Talent Summit, connecting companies with local talent. The Economic Development Team works closely with businesses daily to understand their workforce needs, ensuring access to skilled local talent.

By focusing on retaining local talent, supporting companies in providing quality jobs, and helping residents access educational opportunities, Miami is positioning itself to thrive economically. Through these workforce development initiatives, the city is building a strong foundation for sustainable growth and success.

Sustainability and Resilience

The City of Miami is committed to sustainability and resilience through the Miami Forever Climate Ready strategy, aimed at reducing flood, heat, and storm risks over the next 40 years.

This comprehensive plan focuses on climate adaptation, addressing the impacts of climate change while protecting residents and the environment. Miami, recognized as a global leader in climate adaptation, prioritizes actions that maximize social, environmental, and economic benefits.

The city is enhancing infrastructure, promoting green technologies, and supporting community-based resilience efforts. Regular updates to the strategy ensure ongoing progress in creating a future-ready, climate-resilient Miami for generations to come.

Community Impact

The City of Miami is committed to fostering future development and innovation by encouraging investors to add value to the community, ensuring that growth benefits both residents and businesses.

The Magic City Innovation District (MCID) – Little Haiti is one of Miami’s key economic initiatives, transforming 18 acres of underutilized land into a vibrant hub for innovation, technology, and culture.

This project revitalizes Little Haiti while preserving its cultural identity, creating opportunities for local businesses, entrepreneurs, and residents. With a focus on affordable housing, workforce development, and community engagement, MCID is projected to add $188 million annually to the local economy and generate thousands of jobs.

Additionally, we support local schools like iTech Senior High School by providing resources for future-ready careers. This initiative is a model for inclusive growth, driving both economic vitality and community prosperity in Miami.

Global Competitiveness

The Department of Economic Innovation and Development is committed to positioning the City of Miami as a global leader by leveraging innovation, trade, and investment opportunities.

Through the InvestMiami Program, the department fosters international engagement by hosting business delegations, promoting trade, and building partnerships with global business leaders. These efforts create pathways for local companies to access international markets, driving job creation and economic diversification.

These initiatives also promote knowledge exchange, highlight Miami’s strategic strengths as a business hub, and showcase opportunities in key sectors such as technology, real estate, and trade. By cultivating global business networks, InvestMiami solidifies Miami’s reputation as a premier destination for international commerce and investment.

Population Estimate and Composition

Miami's 2022 population estimate was 449,514, with 49% female and 51% male. 78% of residents speak a language other than English at home.

Education

GDP
