Smart City Initiatives

The Department of Economic Innovation and Development serves as a leader in advancing future-ready initiatives, positioning Miami as an urban testbed for cutting-edge innovation.

Through the Future of Mobility Series, the Department unites thought leaders to explore solutions in transportation and urban mobility. Key projects include:

  • Brickell Digital Twin Demonstration Project: Collaborations with Dell and NVIDIA to use AI and digital simulations for urban optimization and planning.
  • 5G Innovation District: Enhancing connectivity and digital infrastructure in Little River and Little Haiti.

These initiatives solidify Miami’s status as a regional hub for innovation, shaping a future-ready city driven by technology and connectivity.

Economic Growth

The Department of Economic Innovation and Development drives Miami’s economic growth by fostering entrepreneurship, supporting small businesses, and empowering diverse innovators.

Key initiatives include Built in Miami, a business incubator providing essential resources for startups, and YouBelong, a series empowering women entrepreneurs. The department also collaborates with industry leaders like Google, AWS, and JPMorgan to host workshops and networking events.

Partnerships with organizations like Endeavor Miami further support Latin founders, ensuring inclusive growth and innovation while strengthening Miami’s entrepreneurial ecosystem.

Workforce Development

Workforce development is central to Miami’s vision for a thriving, sustainable future.  The City invests in residents through innovative programs and targeted scholarships, empowering local talent and meeting industry needs.

Key initiatives include:

  • Venture Miami Talent Summit: Providing 1:1 career guidance, workshops, and access to hiring opportunities, along with professional development tools like LinkedIn Premium.
  • Venture Miami Tech Hiring Fair: Connecting local talent with top employers, featuring 60+ companies and over 4,000 open roles per event.
  • Venture Miami Scholarship Fund: Bridging educational gaps by offering financial support to at-need residents pursuing STEM degrees with over 100 scholarships awarded to date.

By aligning educational access, job creation, and business needs, Miami is cultivating a skilled workforce and ensuring long-term success for both residents and businesses.

Global Competitiveness

The Department of Economic Innovation and Development is positioning Miami to thrive on the global stage by leveraging innovation, trade, and investment opportunities.

Through the InvestMiami Program, the department hosts business delegations, fosters international partnerships, and connects local companies to global markets, driving job creation and economic diversification.

These efforts highlight Miami’s strategic strengths as a business hub and showcase opportunities in key sectors like finance, technology, real estate, and trade. By building robust global networks, InvestMiami solidifies the city’s reputation as a premier destination for international commerce and investment.

Aviation

Aviation is a key technology sector in Miami, with over 1,800 aviation companies, more than 88,500 local jobs, a regional economic impact of $26 billion, and Miami ranking #1 in international air freight volume and #4 for the best international large airport in the U.S. for passengers.

Technology

Miami's thriving tech ecosystem, featuring over 2,500 startups, 6 unicorns, and $2.41 billion in annual venture capital funding, is ranked #4 nationally for tech job growth. Powered by a $95 billion startup ecosystem, a strategic location bridging North and Latin America, and a strong network of accelerators, incubators, and co-working spaces, Miami has emerged as a global leader in innovation.

Art and Culture

Miami’s creative sector contributes $2.1 billion annually to the economy and supports 31,500 jobs across a wide range of industries. With 100+ cultural institutions, including world-class museums, galleries, and performing arts centers, Miami offers a vibrant cultural landscape that attracts both residents and global visitors.

Life Science & Healthcare 

Miami has become a key player in life sciences and healthcare, with over 100,000 jobs, 1,000+ companies, $4 billion in bioscience sales, and the second-largest medical district in the U.S., driving innovation in biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and health tech while positioning the city as a global hub for transformative healthcare advancements.

Finance 

Miami’s financial sector, home to over 60 international banks and 500+ fintech companies, employs over 150,000 people and generates $28 billion in regional output, positioning the city as the #2 U.S. city for international banks and a vital financial hub for Latin America and the Caribbean.

KEY INDUSTRIES

Trade and Logistics

Miami’s trade and logistics industry is a powerhouse, generating $137 billion annually and supporting over 275,000 jobs, positioning the city as a global commerce hub. Anchored by Miami International Airport (MIA) and PortMiami, the city connects North America to Latin America, the Caribbean, and beyond.

PORT MIAMI

  • Economic Impact: Generates $61.4 billion annually and supports 29,423 jobs.

  • Capacity and Growth: Handles over 1 million TEUs annually, with a $2 billion multimodal upgrade to double cargo capacity in the next decade.

Miami International Airport (MIA)

Top U.S. Airport for International Freight: Handled 2.78 million tons of cargo in 2023.

  • Economic Impact: Contributes $118 billion annually and supports 700,000 jobs locally.

  • Specialized Leadership: Excels in perishable goods, electronics, and pharmaceuticals, with a $400 million expansion in progress

Strategic Assets

  • Warehouse Infrastructure: 277 million square feet of capacity, with an additional 3.9 million square feet under construction in 2024.

  • Free Trade Zones (FTZs): Offer duty exemptions and streamlined trade processes.

  • Logistics Powerhouses: Home to 1,800 companies, including Ryder, FedEx, and Crowley Logistics.

Miami - A Closer Look at Census Data

Miami’s economy is driven by a diverse workforce across key sectors, with a balanced population demographic, strong GDP growth, and a robust school system focused on preparing future talent.

Population Estimate and Composition

Miami's 2023 population estimate was 455,924, with 49% female and 51% male. Approximately 74% of residents speak a language other than English at home.

Education

Miami-Dade County Public Schools, the third-largest district in the U.S., enrolled 335,840 students in 2024-25.

GDP

Miami metropolitan area ranks #1 in Florida by gross domestic product, with approximately $533.67 billion as of 2023.

Workforce Data

Miami’s diverse population is ideal for upskilling and career transitions. In their mid to late 20s, many refine their interests and skills, while midlife (30-50) career changes are driven by shifting priorities and the pursuit of more meaningful work.
