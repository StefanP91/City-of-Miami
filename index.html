Smart City Initiatives

The Department of Economic Innovation and Development is a pioneer in promoting future-ready initiatives in Miami, actively driving smart city initiatives across various sectors.

Through events like the annual Future of Mobility Summit, the department brings together thought leaders and industry experts to explore cutting-edge solutions in transportation and urban mobility.

Additionally, the department supports smart city initiatives by global technology leaders such as Dell and NVIDIA, collaborating on projects like the Brickell Digital Twin Demonstration Project, which uses Dell’s Computer Vision AI Model and NVIDIA Omniverse to analyze the impact of bridge operations on traffic.

The department is also backing the development of a 5G Innovation District in Little River and Little Haiti, further enhancing the city's connectivity and digital infrastructure.

These efforts are crucial in positioning Miami as a hub for innovation, fostering a city that is not only ready for the future but actively shaping it.

Economic Growth

Attracting investments, fostering entrepreneurship, and strengthening the local economy through infrastructure and incentives are key priorities for the Department of Economic Innovation and Development.

The department drives economic growth by supporting small businesses and startups, partnering with industry leaders like Google, AWS, JPMorgan, and Justworks to host networking events and foster collaboration.

Through the Built in Miami Program, a pre-accelerator initiative, and monthly tech mixers at Citizen M, the department provides resources and opportunities for entrepreneurs

Additionally, the department empowers women in business through the YouBelong meetups and supports Latin founders in collaboration with Endeavor Miami.

Workforce Development

Workforce development plays a vital role in Miami’s future.

The City of Miami invests heavily in supporting residents through scholarships and workforce development programs.

Each year, the city hosts the Venture Talent Summit, connecting companies with local talent. The Economic Development Team works closely with businesses daily to understand their workforce needs, ensuring access to skilled local talent.

By focusing on retaining local talent, supporting companies in providing quality jobs, and helping residents access educational opportunities, Miami is positioning itself to thrive economically. Through these workforce development initiatives, the city is building a strong foundation for sustainable growth and success.

Sustainability and Resilience

The City of Miami is committed to sustainability and resilience through the Miami Forever Climate Ready strategy, aimed at reducing flood, heat, and storm risks over the next 40 years.

This comprehensive plan focuses on climate adaptation, addressing the impacts of climate change while protecting residents and the environment. Miami, recognized as a global leader in climate adaptation, prioritizes actions that maximize social, environmental, and economic benefits.

The city is enhancing infrastructure, promoting green technologies, and supporting community-based resilience efforts. Regular updates to the strategy ensure ongoing progress in creating a future-ready, climate-resilient Miami for generations to come.

Aviation

Miami’s trade and logistics industry is a key economic driver, generating $137 billion annually and supporting over 275,000 jobs. Anchored by Miami International Airport (MIA) and PortMiami, the city connects North America to Latin America, the Caribbean, and beyond, solidifying its role as a global commerce hub.

Miami International Airport (MIA):

  • Busiest U.S. airport for international freight, handling 2.78 million tons of cargo in 2023.
  • Supports 700,000 jobs locally and contributes $118 billion annually to the economy.
  • Leading in perishable goods, electronics, and pharmaceuticals, with ongoing expansion through a $400 million Vertically Integrated Cargo Community.

PortMiami:

  • Generates $61.4 billion in economic impact and directly supports 29,423 jobs.
  • Handles over 1 million TEUs annually and is equipped with a $2 billion multimodal upgrade, preparing for doubled cargo volumes in the next decade.

Strategic Assets:

  • 277 million square feet of warehouse capacity, with 3.9 million under construction in 2024.
  • Free Trade Zones (FTZs) offer duty exemptions and streamlined processes.
  • Home to 1,800 logistics companies, including Ryder, FedEx, and Crowley Logistics.

Technology & ICT

Miami’s booming tech ecosystem, with over 2,500 startups, 6 unicorns, $2.41 billion in annual VC funding, and a #4 ranking in tech job growth, has become a global leader in innovation, driven by a $95 billion startup ecosystem, a strategic location connecting North and Latin America, and a robust network of accelerators, incubators, and co-working spaces.

Global Logistics 

Miami’s trade and logistics industry, with $137 billion in annual trade, 9.7 million tons of maritime cargo, and 277 million square feet of warehouse capacity, solidifies its position as the #1 U.S. airport for international freight and top trading partner with Latin America, supported by strategic investments in infrastructure, free trade zones, and top organizations like Ryder, FedEx, and Crowley Logistics.

Art and Culture

Miami’s creative sector contributes $2.1 billion annually to the economy and supports 31,500 jobs across a wide range of industries. With 100+ cultural institutions, including world-class museums, galleries, and performing arts centers, Miami offers a vibrant cultural landscape that attracts both residents and global visitors.

Iconic events like Art Basel position Miami as a global cultural capital, while the city’s thriving culinary scene, featuring 14 Michelin-starred restaurants, showcases its diverse and innovative gastronomy. Premier sports events, from Formula 1 to NBA games, and a dynamic architectural landscape—blending historic Art Deco with cutting-edge modern design—further reinforce its creative identity.

Ranked as the #2 U.S. city for creatives, with an average earning of $91,000, Miami is a magnet for talent and innovation, blending culture, opportunity, and lifestyle into a unique and inspiring destination.

Life Science & Healthcare 

Miami has become a key player in life sciences and healthcare, with over 100,000 jobs, 1,000+ companies, $4 billion in bioscience sales, and the second-largest medical district in the U.S., driving innovation in biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and health tech while positioning the city as a global hub for transformative healthcare advancements.

Finance 

Miami’s financial sector, home to over 60 international banks and 500+ fintech companies, employs over 150,000 people and generates $28 billion in regional output, positioning the city as the #2 U.S. city for international banks and a vital financial hub for Latin America and the Caribbean.

Population Estimate and Composition

Miami's 2022 population estimate was 449,514, with 49% female and 51% male. 78% of residents speak a language other than English at home.

Education
