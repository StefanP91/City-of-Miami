Why Miami?

Discover the dynamic opportunities that make Miami a global leader in innovation, trade, and talent development.

Global Gateway

Miami stands as a strategic link between Latin America, the Caribbean, and the U.S. Key Advantage: Trade and logistics benefits through Miami International Airport and PortMiami.

Innovation Ecosystem

Miami is at the forefront of industry growth in Fintech, Health-tech, and Advanced Mobility.
Key Industries: Tech, healthcare, logistics, and more.

Talent Magnet

A diverse and multilingual workforce supported by world-class universities and training programs.
Why Miami: Access to top-tier talent that drives business success.

Miami’s GDP contribution

  • The City of Miami's GDP is a significant contributor to the broader Miami-Dade economy and it plays a central role in sectors like real estate, finance, and tourism. (Source: Miami-Dade County Economic Impact)
    (Source: Miami-Dade County Economic Impact)

Key Sectors Driving the Economy of the City of Miami (2024):

  • Technology:
    Miami’s tech scene has grown exponentially, with the city now recognized as a prominent tech hub in the U.S. Venture capital investments continued to increase, with Miami securing more than $5 billion in funding for startups.
    (Source: Knight Foundation)
  • Healthcare:
    Healthcare remains one of Miami's largest sectors. Institutions such as the University of Miami Health System and Jackson Memorial Hospital are key drivers of both the local economy and employment.
    (Source: The Firm Miami)
  • Real Estate and Construction:
    Real estate development, particularly in high-end residential and mixed-use properties, is a major economic driver for the City of Miami. Miami has seen a booming real estate market with luxury developments like those in Brickell and Wynwood.
    (Source: The Firm Miami)
  • Logistics and Trade:
    The PortMiami and Miami International Airport (MIA) are key logistics hubs, particularly for trade with Latin America. The city's proximity to international markets makes it a vital link in global trade, with billions of dollars in goods passing through each year.
    (Source: PortMiami)

Miami Industry Growth Trends (2025)

  • Finance & InvestmentMiami
    is becoming a financial hub, attracting hedge funds and investors. The Global Alts conference highlights its growing financial sector.
    (Source: FT)
  • Technology & InnovationGrowth
    in fintech startups (429 in Miami). Projected 13% increase in tech jobs by 2030.
    (Source: Connexionsolutions)
  • Real Estate & Construction
    25% increase in commercial real estate expected. Mixed-use developments transforming neighborhoods (e.g., Brickell, Wynwood).
    (Source: Smith CRE)
  • Tourism & Hospitality
    8% growth in tourism, boosting the economy. Expansion in luxury real estate and tourism infrastructure.
    (Source: Connexionsolutions)
  • Industrial Real Estate Miami
    is a key logistics hub due to its strategic location. Growth driven by the e-commerce boom and strong infrastructure.
    (Source: Agora RE)

Key Employment Metrics for the City of Miami:

  • Total Nonfarm Employment:
    The Miami-Miami Beach-Kendall division added 5,900 jobs, accounting for 57.3% of the metropolitan area's employment gain in the construction industry in November 2024.
    (Source: BLS)
  • Unemployment Rate:
    The unemployment rate for Miami-Dade County was 3.3% as of November 2024.
    (Source: BLS)

Workforce Development Initiatives in the City of Miami:

  • Department of Economic Innovation & Development:Focuses on economic development, technology integration, and workforce empowerment to improve the city's quality of life and attract businesses.
    (Source: City of Miami)

Startup Investments in Miami (2024):

  • Venture Capital:
    Miami saw continued growth in venture capital investments, reaching over $5 billion in recent years.
    (Source: Knight Foundation)
  • Global Ranking:
    Miami ranks 16th globally for startup ecosystems in 2024, up from 23rd in 2023.
    (Source: Beacon Council)

Startup Success Rates (2024):

  • Ecosystem Performance:
    Miami ranks among the top 30 global ecosystems, reflecting strong startup performance.
    (Source: Refresh Miami)
  • Early-Stage Funding:
    Ranked in the top 15 North American cities for early-stage funding.
    (Source: Refresh Miami)
